The Signals

Sig5 covers 8 instruments: US30, NAS100, US500, JPN225, GER40, BTCUSD, XAUUSD, and XAGUSD.

Signal frequency varies by market conditions. On active days you may see 2–4 signals across all instruments. There will also be days with no signals — the system only fires when a valid setup is confirmed.

Every entry signal is delivered as a JSON payload to your webhook URL:

{
  "id":     "A3F9C2-B7D-US30",
  "ticker": "US30",
  "action": "BUY",
  "price":  40812,
  "sl":     40512,
  "volume": 0.33
}

When a trade closes, an exit signal is sent:

{
  "id":     "A3F9C2-B7D-US30",
  "ticker": "US30",
  "action": "CLOSE"
}

The id field is unique per subscriber per signal. volume is your lot size, calculated automatically based on your drawdown limit and current risk level.

Signals can fire during any active market session — London, New York, and Asian sessions depending on the instrument. There is no fixed schedule; signals fire when the strategy conditions are met.

High-impact news events are monitored in real time. Signals are blocked automatically during high-impact events within the relevant session window.

Risk & Lot Sizing

Your lot size is calculated from your drawdown limit and your current risk percentage:

risk_amount = drawdown_limit × (current_risk_pct / 100)
lots        = risk_amount / stop_distance

Example: $10,000 drawdown limit at 1.0% risk on a US30 long with a 300pt stop = 0.33 lots. Stop distances are fixed per instrument — your drawdown limit is the only input required.

The decay ladder automatically adjusts your risk percentage after each trade outcome. After a loss, risk decays by 15%. After a win, it recovers by 33%. Risk is capped at 1.0% and never drops below 0.25% regardless of consecutive losses. This is the same risk system running on the live portfolio behind Sig5.

Enter your account's maximum allowed drawdown in USD. For example, if you have a $100K account with a 10% maximum drawdown, enter 10000. Do not inflate this figure — it directly controls your position sizing and an inflated value will produce oversized trades that may breach your account rules.

No. Lot sizes are calculated automatically by the system. Your current risk percentage is read-only and managed by the decay ladder. Your drawdown limit is the only value you control.

Technical Setup

A webhook URL is an endpoint provided by your broker or copy tool that listens for incoming trade instructions. You typically find it in your platform settings under something like "webhooks", "incoming signals", or "API integration". Refer to your platform's documentation for the exact location.

Any tool that accepts a JSON webhook POST request. WebhookTrade and Copygram are commonly used options, but any equivalent service works. Many brokers running MT5 also support webhook execution natively via plugins — check your broker's documentation. If your tool has a webhook input field, it is compatible with Sig5.

Sig5 makes one delivery attempt per signal. If your webhook is unreachable, the signal will not be retried. It is your responsibility to ensure your webhook URL remains active and reachable. Delivery success is logged and visible in your dashboard.
